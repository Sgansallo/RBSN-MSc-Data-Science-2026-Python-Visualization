

#  S&P 500 Time Series Analysis (Python EDA & Visualization)

## Overview

This project explores the historical performance of the **S&P 500 stock market index** using Python. It focuses on **exploratory data analysis (EDA)** and **data visualization** to understand long-term market trends, volatility, and trading behavior.

The dataset spans from **1950 to present**, providing a rich time series for financial analysis and insights.


## Objectives

* Analyze long-term S&P 500 market trends
* Understand price movement patterns over time
* Study volatility and trading volume behavior
* Explore relationships between key market indicators
* Build clear and meaningful visualizations for insights

---

## 📂 Dataset Description

The dataset includes daily market data with the following features:

* **Date** – Trading date
* **Open** – Opening price
* **High** – Highest price of the day
* **Low** – Lowest price of the day
* **Close** – Closing price
* **Volume** – Number of shares traded
* **Dividends** – Dividend payments
* **Stock Splits** – Stock split events

##  Tools & Libraries

* Python 
* Pandas
* NumPy
* Matplotlib
* Seaborn
* Google Colab / Jupyter Notebook


## Key Analysis Performed

* Historical trend analysis of S&P 500 Close price
* Moving averages for trend smoothing
* Daily and long-term return analysis
* Volume vs price behavior
* Market volatility exploration
* Correlation between financial indicators


## Visualizations

This project includes:

* Line plots for long-term trends
* Moving average charts
* Distribution plots for returns
* Volume trend analysis
* Correlation heatmaps
* Volatility patterns over time


## Key Insights

* The S&P 500 shows a strong long-term upward trend
* Market volatility varies significantly across time periods
* Trading volume often increases during high volatility periods
* Financial indicators show meaningful relationships over time
